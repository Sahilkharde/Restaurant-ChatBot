Identity Information – Your name, organization, and location.
Public Key – Used for encryption and creating digital signatures.
Serial Number – A unique identifier for the certificate.
Validity Period – The duration for which the certificate is valid.

Key reasons a digital certificate is required:
Identity Verification – Confirms the notary is an authorized and commissioned official.
Digital Signatures – Enables creation of secure, legally binding electronic signatures.
Document Integrity – Protects documents from tampering or unauthorized modifications.
Regulatory Compliance – Meets federal and state requirements (e.g., ESIGN Act, URPERA).
Trust & Security – Assures signers, businesses, and government agencies that the notarization is authentic and valid.
Without a digital certificate, a notary cannot securely perform Remote Online Notarizations, as it is the core element linking the notary’s identity to their electronic seal and signature.

Q: Can I notarize a document for someone in another state?

A: If you suspect the signer is not acting voluntarily or is under duress, you must refuse to notarize the document. As a notary, you are responsible for ensuring the signer is acting of their own free will.

Q: What happens if a mistake is made during the notarization process?
A: If a mistake is made during the notarization process, such as signing the wrong document or recording incorrect information, you should immediately notify the signer, correct the mistake, and re-notarize the document if needed.

A: No, eNotary On Call is not responsible for the content of the documents being notarized. It is the notary’s responsibility to ensure the document meets legal requirements and that the signer is fully aware of the content being notarized.

Q: Is there a reason why I am not receiving any requests for notarizations?
A: There are several reasons you might not be receiving requests:
Availability Status – Ensure you are logged into your notary account and marked as available to receive requests.
Location Sharing – When accepting requests via the mobile app, you must share your location quickly after clicking “Accept.” If the location isn’t shared in time, the request may not be assigned.
Competition Among Notaries – Multiple notaries often receive the same request simultaneously. Faster responses and confirmations usually secure the assignment.
Subscription Plan – Open customer requests require an active subscription plan to receive them. Direct customer assignments do not require a subscription.
Tip: Stay logged into both the mobile app (for instant notifications) and your desktop account (for completing notarizations) to maximize your chances of receiving and accepting requests.
